# Marketing Examples

> User journeys, personas, and layout patterns from the BookaBox marketing playbook. Distilled from `230719_Marketing.pdf` (Stand Juli 2023). Original PDF available in Drive (file ID `1q8u-ys-HuXugy4BpCJqacWMKDQH1Y5Dj`) for visual reference.

This file documents how the brand has historically gone to market across paid, owned, and earned channels — and which audience segments map to which storage size and use case.

## User journeys — three canonical paths

### Path 1: Klassische Anzeige → Website
Print or display ad with a clear CTA (logo + URL) → user lands on `bookabox.com` Startseite → from there into product / location overview.

Use when: brand-led top-of-funnel, when reach matters more than direct conversion, OOH placements with no scannable element.

### Path 2: Social Media → Onepager → Detail
Social ad or organic post → tappable link to a campaign-specific Onepager → from there to Website Location Overview or Product Detailseite.

Use when: campaign or product launch, when you can target a specific persona, when measurement matters (link can be tracked).

### Path 3: Webbanner → Onepager → Detail
Display banner (animated or static) → Onepager → Location/Product Detail.

Use when: retargeting, regional campaigns, partner placements (e.g., on a media outlet's site).

## Onepager structure

The campaign Onepager is a single-page landing template. Canonical structure (from marketing PDF):

1. **Visual / Headline** — the hook, taken from the campaign creative
2. **Individual content** (e.g., a quote, a testimonial, a gallery)
3. **Einzelprodukt** — the specific box size or use case being pushed
4. **Produktrange / Produktübersicht** — wider context, related options
5. **Content + Testimonials** — social proof
6. **CTA** — booking, location finder, or contact

Two Onepager variants are mentioned: one for product launches, one for location openings. Both follow the same skeleton; the campaign creative changes the top.

## Service messaging — the six-feature box

The marketing playbook frames the service offering through six features. These are the things the brand routinely talks about:

1. **All-in-one App** — Buchung, Verwaltung, Zugang, Support in einer App
2. **Paketservice** — Pakete werden direkt zur Box geliefert (BAB Parcel System)
3. **Interior** — durchdachtes Innendesign, modulare Räume, Co-Working-Optionen
4. **Sicherheit** — Zugangskontrolle, Überwachung, Anomalie-Erkennung (in der aktuellen Generation: Face Recognition, AI-basiert)
5. **Flex Tarif** — flexible Vertragslaufzeiten, Größenanpassung, Stornierung
6. **Onlinebuchung** — vollständig digital, ohne Termin, sofortiger Zugang

When listing features, this is the canonical order. When prioritizing for a specific audience, pick the two or three that matter most — don't list all six.

## Cross-cutting themes

Beyond the standard product/persona work, the brand has two recurring campaign themes worth knowing:

### Regionalität (Standorte)
Local launch campaigns tied to a specific city. These lean into Pole A — emotional / edgy headlines that play with local identity. *"Hallo Braunschweig!"*, *"Endlich auch in Bochum"*, the Jägermeister joke for Braunschweig (which is the home of Jägermeister).

### Promis / Events / Comedians
Co-branded or testimonial-led campaigns. Examples mentioned:
- Jägermeister (Braunschweig regional tie-in)
- Comedians (e.g., "Teddy" referenced in marketing PDF)
- Events with cultural figures

For these, the headline is often the testimonial itself or a play on the celebrity persona. Pole A territory.

### Wall question — locations
At locations, an interior typography pattern poses the question:

> **"Was lagerscht du?"**

This is a regional dialect spelling (Mallorca / Bavarian / playful) of "Was lagerst du?" — meant to disarm and make the customer feel at home. Use as wall typography, social hashtag, or campaign hook.

## Layout patterns from this playbook

The marketing PDF establishes several composition patterns the brand reuses:

### Visual + Headline (top), Content + CTA (bottom)
Standard split for posters, social cards, OOH. Visual occupies the upper 60–70%, the headline sits over or under the visual, content and CTA below.

### Persona testimonial card
Photo of one of the 11 motives + the persona's first-person line + the wordmark at the bottom.

> Ich bin Galeristin. Den nötigen Platz für meine Kunst bietet mir BookaBox.com.

This pattern works at building scale (wall typography), in social, and in print.

### Size comparison ladder
1m, 2m, 3m sizes shown side-by-side with consistent visual treatment. Used for the kiosk screen, the Find-Your-Box flow, and product detail pages. This is a Pole B device — calm, comparative, factual.

### Greeting + news
*"Hallo [Stadt]! Neueröffnung am [Datum]."* — used for location launches. Pole A. Warm, direct, local.

## How to use this file

When planning a campaign, briefing a designer, or writing copy:

1. Pick the **persona / size combo** from the table.
2. Pick the **user journey path** (1, 2, or 3) based on objective and channel.
3. Decide on **Pole A or Pole B** for the headline (see `voice-and-tone.md`).
4. Pick **one or two** of the six features to emphasize — not all six.
5. If there's a regional or cultural angle, decide whether to lean on it (Regionalität / Promi / Event theme).
6. Use the Onepager skeleton as the destination structure.
