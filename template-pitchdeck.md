# Pitch Deck Pattern

> Slide-deck layout pattern for investor, partner, sales, and strategy decks. Codified from the BookaBox.com Fund deck (April 2026), aligned with the brandbook 230918. Use this as a starting point for any BookaBox slide deck longer than ~5 slides.

For one-off slides, social carousels, or short product walkthroughs, this pattern is overkill — use simpler templates.

## Format

- **Aspect ratio:** 16:9 widescreen
- **Page count:** numbered, shown bottom-right as `NN / TOTAL` (e.g., `07 / 15`)
- **Slide ID:** every slide has a number top-left in the format `№ 01`, with the number reversed-out on a campaign-color block
- **Section label:** top-right, mono caps, e.g., `APRIL 2026 · CONFIDENTIAL` or `OPERATOR PROFILE · 2 OF 3`
- **Footer:** `BookaBox.com` bottom-left, optional source line center-bottom in mono caps

## Slide backgrounds — two registers

The deck alternates between two background treatments. Both are on-brand. Pick one per slide based on what the slide is doing.

### Register A — Black canvas (statement / hero)
- Background: Brand Black `#000000`
- Body text: Brand White `#FFFFFF`
- Highlight: campaign color (one phrase, one number, one word)
- Use for: cover, section dividers, big-claim slides, thank-you slide, statement slides where one idea must dominate

### Register B — Light canvas (analytical / dense)
- Background: Brand White `#FFFFFF` or `#F5F5F5` (off-white)
- Body text: Brand Black `#000000`
- Highlight: campaign color (one accent — a number, a verdict, one keyword)
- Use for: data slides, comparison tables, playbooks, deal structures, anything dense

**Do not mix both registers on one slide.** Don't put black blocks on the light layout or light cards on the black layout — the deck reads as alternating chapters, not as a mosaic.

## Typography hierarchy

- **Slide headline:** **Silka Bold**, very large (display size), left-aligned, max 2 lines. Often uses the campaign color on the second line or on one keyword for emphasis. (Earlier drafts said Silka Black — corrected to Bold per brandbook S. 7.)
- **Subtitle / kicker:** mono caps, small, above or below the headline. Example: `WHY OPERATOR-LED WINS` or `OPERATOR PROFILE · 1 OF 3`
- **Body:** Silka Regular, comfortable reading size. Short paragraphs (2–3 lines max).
- **Labels (column heads, table heads, axis labels):** mono caps, small, gray
- **Big numbers:** Silka Bold, oversized (3–6× body size). Always paired with one short label below. Use Silka Black only for genuine extra-emphasis cases.
- **Source / footer:** mono caps, very small, gray

## Headline patterns that work

The Fund deck uses a recurring rhetorical move: **two beats, where the second beat reframes the first.** Reuse this pattern.

Examples from the deck:

- *"Some buildings are sitting empty. We don't think they should be."* (problem → stance)
- *"Buy at office cap rates. Stabilise at storage cap rates."* (action → outcome)
- *"Two markets. Two repricings. One window."* (setup → punchline)
- *"Not only a box. An infrastructure."* (denial → upgrade)
- *"You're not let in. You're welcomed."* (negation → reframe)
- *"Owns the building. Runs the business inside."* (parallel → escalation)

**Pattern recipe:** Sentence A. Sentence B that reframes A in the brand's favor. Both sentences short. Period after each. The campaign color usually lands on the second sentence or one keyword inside it.

## Layout building blocks

Use these as the vocabulary. Each slide is composed of one or two of these — never more than three.

### 1. Stat card
A single oversized number with a one-line label. Used for headline metrics: `5.5×`, `€1.25M`, `9 locations`, `+5% YoY`.
Format: number in Silka Bold, label in mono caps small below.

### 2. Side-by-side comparison
Two columns, equal width. Each column has a header, a stat, and a short bullet list.
Markers: use `×` (cross) for the losing/old side, `→` (arrow) for the winning/new side. Avoid `✓` and `✗`.
Example: pure RE fund (×) vs. operator-led fund (→).

### 3. Data list with provenance
Vertical bar-chart-as-text. Each row: label, bar, value. Always cite source in the footer.
Example: self-storage sqft per capita by country.

### 4. Step / playbook strip
Numbered horizontal sequence: 1 → 2 → 3 → 4. Each step has a short title (mono caps), a 2-line description, and optional milestone label below.
Use for: timelines, processes, playbooks.

### 5. Three-column profile
Three columns, often roman-numeralled (I, II, III). Each column has a kicker, a short headline, and 3–4 sub-points. Used for breaking a topic into facets.
Example: "Customer Experience / Security / Operating Intelligence" on the AI Operating System slide.

### 6. Worked example / split
A single building / object / scenario on top, two outcomes branching below (e.g., landlord side / operator side), summed in a combined total. Used for deal-economics slides.

### 7. Photo block
Full-bleed or half-bleed photograph. Caption in mono caps top-left. Optional black bar overlay with a single line of text.

## Campaign color discipline

Hard Rule #7 from `brand-guide.md` applies. **One campaign color per deck.** Pick one at the start and use it as the single highlight throughout. Do not introduce a second color for variety.

### Default choices for BookaBox decks

- **Brand Coral Pink `#F25966`** — default for consumer-facing, retail, brand, and most BookaBox storytelling decks. The dominant brand-warm color. Use this unless you have a specific reason to use one of the alternatives.
- **Functional Orange `#FF9829`** — institutional / financial / investor framing (Fund deck precedent — April 2026)
- **Brand Bright Blue `#00A0E0`** — informational / operational / technology-focused decks
- **Functional Red `#D6454C`** — confident, alert tone — for crisis comms or urgency-driven messaging

For most decks, **start with Coral Pink** and only switch if the audience and topic clearly call for a different feel.

Within a slide, the campaign color highlights **exactly one thing**:
- the punchline word in a headline, OR
- one big stat, OR
- one keyword in a body line, OR
- one bar in a chart

Not all four on the same slide.

## Source attribution

Investor and partner decks need source lines. Footer in mono caps gray, separated by `·`:

- `FEDESSA / CBRE EUROPEAN SELF STORAGE INDUSTRY REPORT 2025 · 1,447 STORES · 16 COUNTRIES`
- `INDUSTRY OPEX: SHURGARD 2024 (~33%) · PUBLIC STORAGE 2024 (~36%) · SAFESTORE 2024 (~34%)`

If a number is internal/illustrative, label it `ILLUSTRATIVE` in the kicker. Never put internal estimates next to cited industry numbers without that label.

## Voice in pitch decks

Pitch decks lean into the **descriptive/informative** pole — short, declarative, evidence-led. The edgy/expressive pole is used sparingly, mostly on the cover and section dividers, where one strong claim sets up the analytical content that follows.

For formal/institutional/investor-facing decks, use the formal claim variant **"Mach' Dir Dein Leben leichter."** (see `brand-guide.md` Hard Rule). For consumer-facing decks (sales, brand, internal strategy), use the standard **"Mach' Dir Dein Leben einfacher."**

Customer-facing rules still apply where the audience is addressed:
- If the deck speaks to "you" the reader, the brand still uses Du (capitalized) in German
- The wordmark stays "BookaBox.com" in Silka Bold — including in source lines and footers

## Logo on slides

Use the **wordmark** (not the pill) in the deck footer. The pill variant is appropriate as a CTA element in the closing slide if a call-to-action is present.

## Closing slide

Mirror the cover: black canvas, the wordmark large, one line of brand promise. The Fund deck closes with the formal-context claim variant *"Mach' Dir Dein Leben leichter."*

## What to avoid

- Don't put more than one big stat per slide
- Don't use bullet lists longer than 4 items
- Don't combine Register A (black) and Register B (light) elements on one slide
- Don't introduce icons in colors other than the chosen campaign color or grayscale
- Don't decorate — every visual element should carry information
- Don't use stock-photo people; use the canonical 8 portrait motives from `imagery-index.md`
- Don't use Silka Black where Silka Bold is the canonical headline weight
- Don't use both Coral Pink and Orange in the same deck — pick one
