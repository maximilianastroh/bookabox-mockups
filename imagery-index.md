## Personas mentioned in the marketing materials

These are the customer segments BookaBox has defined. Each likely corresponds to one or more of the 8 motives above, plus the box-size renderings on brandbook S. 11.

- **Student / Norman** — kleine Fläche (1–3 m²), Privatkunde, urbaner Kontext
- **Das Paar / 2-Personen-Haushalt** — mittlere Fläche (3–5 m²), Lebensumbruch (Umzug, Familienzuwachs, Renovierung)
- **Galeristin** — kreative Berufe, Lagerung von Kunst, ~8 m²
- **Abenteurer / Sportler** — kleine bis mittlere Fläche (3 m²+), Hobby-Equipment (Fahrräder, Skier, Surfboards)
- **Gewerbe / Akten** — Büro-/Aktenlagerung, kleine Fläche (1 m²+)
- **E-Commerce Händler "Fitness"** — Gewerbe, größere Fläche (10–15 m²), Warenlager
- **Handwerker** — Gewerbe, ~15 m², Werkzeug- und Materiallager
- **Promis / Comedians** — Imagebildung, Co-Branding (z. B. Teddy, Jägermeister-Beispiel)

## How to brief imagery

When briefing a designer or asking for a specific image:

1. **Specify the motiv by number:** "Use Motiv 4 (drag queen)" — not "the one with the wig"
2. **Specify the campaign color** for any text or graphic overlay (this matches the motiv's background)
3. **Specify the surface** — this determines crop, resolution, treatment
4. **Stay in one persona per piece** — don't mix Motiv 2 (sportsman) with Motiv 7 (office worker) on the same surface unless the concept explicitly compares personas
5. **The Coral Pink motiv (Motiv 4)** is the iconic BookaBox image — use it for hero campaigns, brand-story pieces, key social posts

## Product imagery (separate from people)

Product imagery — boxes, kiosk, app screens, location interiors — is not catalogued by motiv numbers but follows its own brand standards:

- Clean, well-lit, real (not rendered for marketing — note that brandbook S. 11 uses isometric 3D renderings, those are fine for size-reference)
- Branded surfaces visible (wordmark, boxnumber)
- Interior shots use the location color palette + materials defined in `tokens.md` § Color — Interior

For product imagery in pitch decks and partner materials, the BookaBox.com Fund deck (April 2026) is a good reference for how interior shots and product photography are composed.
