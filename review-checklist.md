# Review Checklist

> Use this when reviewing existing BookaBox work — copy, design, campaigns, layouts. Walk through every section. Do not skip. Findings are organized into three tiers at the end.

## Section 1 — Strategic anchor

- [ ] Does this piece make a customer's life easier, or visibly demonstrate that BookaBox does?
- [ ] Does it reduce complexity rather than add to it?
- [ ] Does it foreground at least one of the five values (Einfachheit, Zuverlässig, Empathie, Gemeinschaft, Autonomie)?
- [ ] If it pushes a feature, is the feature framed as a *benefit to the customer*, not a brand achievement?

## Section 2 — Address & language

- [ ] Consumer-facing German: customer addressed as "Du", capitalized "Dir / Dein / Dich"?
- [ ] No accidental "Sie" forms in consumer copy?
- [ ] Landlord / B2B / press copy: register consistent throughout (Du or Sie, not both)?
- [ ] English copy: "you" lowercase, natural English phrasing (not translated German)?

## Section 3 — The claim

- [ ] If a claim line appears, is it exactly one of the two sanctioned variants:
  - **"Mach' Dir Dein Leben einfacher."** (consumer default)
  - **"Mach' Dir Dein Leben leichter."** (formal / institutional)
- [ ] No invented variants ("Wir machen", "Machen wir", "We make", etc.)?
- [ ] If translated to English, is the spirit preserved without inventing a new official line?

## Section 4 — Wordmark

- [ ] Spelled exactly **"BookaBox.com"** — camelCase, with ".com"?
- [ ] No accidental "Bookabox", "BookaBox" without suffix, "BOOKABOX", or "BookABox"?
- [ ] Set in Silka (or correctly substituted only where the medium forces it)?

## Section 5 — Typography

- [ ] Silka used throughout? (No Helvetica, Arial, Inter, Montserrat unless emergency-flagged)
- [ ] Hierarchy clear: headline (Silka Black) > body (Silka Regular) > labels (Silka Medium / mono caps)?
- [ ] Font weights from the brand set only (Black, Bold, Medium, Regular, Light + italics)?
- [ ] No display weight invented or substituted?

## Section 6 — Color discipline

- [ ] Built in black and grayscale as the base?
- [ ] **One** campaign color per piece, used as a single highlight?
- [ ] The campaign color emphasizes exactly **one** thing per slide / page / panel — not two or three?
- [ ] No off-brand hex codes introduced?
- [ ] Functional colors (system green for success, system red for error) used only in their functional role?

## Section 7 — Imagery

- [ ] If people appear, are they from the 11 canonical motives (Motiv1–Motiv11)?
- [ ] No stock-photo people?
- [ ] Product imagery clean, well-lit, real (not rendered), branded surfaces visible?
- [ ] If an interior shot of a location, does it show the interior color palette (base + materials) consistently?

## Section 8 — Voice and tone

- [ ] Has one pole been chosen — Emotional/Edgy *or* Descriptive/Informative — and held throughout?
- [ ] Sentences short (most under 15 words)?
- [ ] No empty intensifiers (wirklich, absolut, 100%, best, beste)?
- [ ] No corporate buzzwords (Synergien, ganzheitlich, kundenzentriert, enabler)?
- [ ] If Pole A: does the headline do the work, with minimal supporting copy?
- [ ] If Pole B: is the message clear, factual, and free of tonal twists?

## Section 9 — Headline structure (if a headline is present)

- [ ] Does the headline match one of the canonical patterns?
  - HEADLINE alone
  - HEADLINE + SUBLINE
  - SKYLINE + HEADLINE
  - HEADLINE + "Logosatz"
  - Wall typography (location only)
  - For pitch decks: Sentence A. Sentence B that reframes A.
- [ ] Is it short enough to read in one beat?
- [ ] If a punchline word is meant to land, is it isolated (not buried in a long line)?

## Section 10 — Layout & composition

- [ ] White space respected — not crammed?
- [ ] Single focal point per surface?
- [ ] Wordmark placement consistent with brand guide?
- [ ] If multi-surface (e.g., social carousel, deck), does the same composition logic repeat across slides?

## Section 11 — Pitch-deck-specific (only if reviewing a slide deck)

- [ ] Slide numbering format: `№ 01` top-left, `NN / TOTAL` bottom-right?
- [ ] Section labels in mono caps top-right?
- [ ] One register per slide (black canvas OR light canvas — not mixed)?
- [ ] One big stat per slide maximum?
- [ ] Bullet lists capped at 4 items?
- [ ] Source attribution in mono caps at footer for any cited number?
- [ ] Internal/illustrative numbers labeled `ILLUSTRATIVE` in the kicker?

## Section 12 — Channel fit

- [ ] Does this surface use the right pole for its medium? (See voice-and-tone.md tone calibration table)
- [ ] If social: hook in first 3 seconds / first line?
- [ ] If email: subject line passes the same brand bar as a headline?
- [ ] If OOH: legible at distance / from a moving vehicle / on small screens?

## Findings — three tiers

After walking through all sections, organize findings:

### Blockers (must fix before shipping)
Brand-breaking issues. Wrong logo use. Wrong typeface. Wrong claim variant. Strategic mismatch with the values. "Sie" in consumer copy. Stock-photo people. Off-pole tonal mix.

### Improvements (on-brand but could be stronger)
Specific, actionable suggestions. The piece works but the headline could be tighter, the campaign color could be applied to the right word, the bullet list could shrink from 6 to 3.

### Optional polish (stylistic nits, minor wins)
Punctuation. Spacing. A small phrasing upgrade. Things that make the piece slightly better but won't be missed if the team is short on time.

## Verdict

End every review with one of three verdicts:
- **Ship it** — no blockers, optional polish only.
- **Ship after fixing blockers** — clear list of must-fixes; no rework needed beyond those.
- **Needs rework** — multiple blockers, strategic mismatch, or fundamental brand drift. Send back for redraft.

For every blocker and every improvement, **quote the specific brand rule being applied** (which section of `brand-guide.md` or `voice-and-tone.md`), and **propose a concrete fix**. Do not just say "the tone is off" — show what to change and why.
