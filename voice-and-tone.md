# Voice and Tone

> Detailed voice rules and headline patterns for BookaBox.com. Distilled from `230918_BaB-Brandbook.pdf` (Stand 18. September 2023), supplemented by examples from the BookaBox.com Fund deck (April 2026) and the OOH/social mockups in the brandbook (S. 13–17, 19–22, 25, 47).

The brand has one identity but speaks in two registers depending on context. Before writing, decide which register fits — then commit to it for the whole piece.

## The two poles

### Pole A — Emotional / Expressiv / Edgy

Sharp, witty, observational. Plays with everyday situations the audience recognizes. Often uses a setup-and-punchline structure or an unexpected angle. Self-aware about being a storage brand. The headline does the work; supporting copy is minimal.

**Use this pole for:**
- Out-of-home (posters, billboards, signage at locations)
- Social media campaigns where reach and memorability matter
- Brand-building above-the-line work
- Local launch announcements when you want personality
- Anything competing for attention against unrelated content

**Examples (canonical, from brandbook OOH pages):**
- *"Dein Schrank ist zu klein für zwei Persönlichkeiten?"* (S. 13, drag queen on Coral Pink — the flagship campaign)
- *"Euer Einhorn wird zu groß für die Garage?"* (S. 14, on Sand)
- *"Deine Akten werden langsam zum Akt?"* (S. 15, on Soft Pink)
- *"10 Jahre Aufbewahrungspflicht, 10m² Home Office?"* (S. 16, on Bright Blue)
- *"1st Class Lager für Deinen 2nd Hand Shop?"* (S. 17, on Dusty Rose)
- *"Erste eigene Bude, kein zweites Zimmer?"* (S. 22, web banner on Webbanner Green)
- *"Hallo Braunschweig! Neueröffnung am 01. September."*
- *"Smart storage neu gedacht. Endlich auch in Bochum."*
- *"Ich sorge für Ordnung in unserem Büro / Unsere Akten lagern bei BookaBox.com"*

**Pattern recipes:**
- **Setup → twist:** start with an everyday scene, end with a wink. The audience finishes the joke in their head. *"Dein Schrank ist zu klein für zwei Persönlichkeiten?"* sets up a closet-space joke; the punchline is the audience realizing this is a storage company speaking metaphorically about identity.
- **Question form:** the brandbook's flagship campaigns are mostly questions. *"Werden langsam zum Akt?", "Zu groß für die Garage?"* — let the reader recognize themselves.
- **Greeting + news:** *Hallo [Stadt]! [Sache passiert].* Direct, warm, makes the launch feel personal.
- **Comparative reframing:** *X neu gedacht. Endlich auch in [Stadt].* Modesty + arrival.
- **First-person testimonial:** the customer speaks; the brand is named at the end. Used at locations on wall typography ("Ich bin Galeristin. Den nötigen Platz für meine Kunst bietet mir BookaBox.com.").

### Pole B — Descriptive / Informative

Calm, clear, functional. The headline tells you what is on offer or how it works. No twist, no joke, no double meaning. The audience already has intent — the copy just confirms they're in the right place.

**Use this pole for:**
- Website (product pages, FAQ, terms, location overview)
- Onboarding and in-app copy
- How-to guides, FAQs, support
- Email confirmations and operational comms
- Investor and partner pitch decks (institutional register)
- Press releases (news copy, factual tone)
- Newsletter announcements where the news is the value
- Landlord and B2B communication

**Examples (canonical):**
- *"Smart. In 3 Klicks zur optimalen Lagerlösung."*
- *"Wir bieten alles, was Du rund ums Lagern brauchst."*
- *"Dein smarter Lagerraum – unbegrenzter Komfort, deine Regeln"* (live website)

**Examples (from Fund deck — formal-institutional sub-register of Pole B):**
- *"Buy at office cap rates. Stabilise at storage cap rates."*
- *"Two markets. Two repricings. One window."*
- *"Not only a box. An infrastructure."*
- *"You're not let in. You're welcomed."*
- *"Owns the building. Runs the business inside."*

**Pattern recipes:**
- **Adjective. Sentence.** *"Smart. In 3 Klicks zur optimalen Lagerlösung."* The adjective is the promise; the sentence is the proof.
- **Sentence A. Sentence B that reframes A in the brand's favor.** Both short. Period after each. Used heavily in pitch decks.
- **Negation → reframe.** *"Not only a box. An infrastructure."* / *"You're not let in. You're welcomed."*
- **Parallel → escalation.** *"Owns the building. Runs the business inside."*

## Wall typography (S. 47) — at locations

A distinct in-physical-space surface with its own conventions. These are the canonical wall-typography patterns:

- **"Wo Dinge wohnen"** — the location-level master claim, in massive Silka Bold across a long wall
- **"Quadratmieter bei BookaBox.com"** — tagline, on dark anthrazit strip at top of wall
- **"Move, store, move, repeat."** — secondary motto, on lighter strip
- **Lists of names**: artist names (Van Gogh, Matisse, Rembrandt, Picasso, Monet, da Vinci, Vermeer) paired with **lists of objects** (Fahrrad, Sofa, Couch, Esstisch, Lampe, Stuhl, Kommode) — set in Silka Bold, vertical lists, juxtaposed for contrast
- **First-person testimonial format** ("Ich bin Galeristin. / Den nötigen Platz für meine / Kunst bietet mir / BookaBox.com.") — set in massive Silka Bold

The wall typography is **not subtitled** — the location itself is the context. Single nouns and verbs at architectural scale ("Move", "Help", "Deliver") are the whole message.

## Hard rules across both poles

1. **The customer is "Du" in German, capitalized "Dir / Dein / Dich".** Never "Sie" in consumer-facing copy. Landlord / B2B / press copy may use "Sie" when the convention demands — flag this and stay consistent within the document.
2. **The wordmark is "BookaBox.com".** Always camelCase, always with ".com". Never write "Bookabox" or "BookaBox" without the suffix.
3. **The social avatar is "BaB"** — lowercase `a` in the middle (B-a-B). Never "BoB" with a lowercase `o`. The abbreviation is **B**ook**a**Box.
4. **The claim is "Mach' Dir Dein Leben einfacher."** Consumer-facing, default. The formal variant **"Mach' Dir Dein Leben leichter."** is sanctioned for institutional / investor / formal contexts. No other variants ("Wir machen", "Machen wir") are sanctioned.
5. **Headlines are set in Silka Bold** (not Silka Black, despite earlier draft assumptions). Silka Black is reserved for special heavier emphasis. See `tokens.md` § Typography for the corrected hierarchy.
6. **Short over long.** When a sentence runs past 15 words, break it in two. The brand sounds confident because it does not over-explain.
7. **No empty intensifiers.** Avoid "wirklich", "absolut", "100%ig", "the best", "die beste", "unschlagbar". These are filler words that weaken the claim. State the thing directly.
8. **No corporate buzzwords.** No "Synergien", "ganzheitlich", "kundenzentriert", "best-in-class", "enabler". If the same idea can be said in plain German, say it that way.

## Tone calibration by surface

| Surface | Pole | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Out-of-home poster / billboard | A | Headline does the work. Pill-CTA + Coral Pink (or other highlight) background. |
| Location signage (interior) | A or B | A for wall typography ("Wo Dinge wohnen"); B for wayfinding ("Find your Box") |
| Website hero | A or B | Brand-led pages → A. Conversion-led pages (booking, pricing) → B. |
| Website product detail | B | Calm, factual, no twist |
| Booking flow / app onboarding | B | Reduce cognitive load. The voice is invisible. |
| Email confirmation | B | Functional; "Du" stays warm without being chatty |
| Newsletter | A or B | Editorial pieces → A. News digest → B. |
| Social media (Instagram) | A | Personality, observation, hook. Coral Pink is the brand-warm default. |
| Social media (LinkedIn) | B with a touch of A | Professional but not corporate |
| Press release | B | News copy. Quotes from founders may use A. |
| Pitch deck (investor / partner) | B (institutional sub-register) | Short, declarative, evidence-led. Cover and dividers may borrow from A. |
| Landlord communication | B | Sie-form acceptable; formal but not stiff |
| Web banner ad | A | Short, punchy. Often Webbanner Green or Coral Pink. |

## Headline patterns (canonical)

The brandbook's flagship campaigns reveal recurring structures. Reuse these:

1. **The recognition question** — *"Dein Schrank ist zu klein für zwei Persönlichkeiten?"* / *"Euer Einhorn wird zu groß für die Garage?"* / *"Werden Deine Akten langsam zum Akt?"* — pose a question that the audience recognizes from their own life. The implicit answer is "yes, and BookaBox solves it."
2. **HEADLINE alone** — single line, expressive, often a question or observation
3. **HEADLINE + SUBLINE** — main claim plus context. *"Hallo Braunschweig! / Neueröffnung am 01. September. / Jägermeister für alle."*
4. **SKYLINE + HEADLINE** — kicker on top, claim below. *"Smart storage / neu gedacht. / Endlich auch in Bochum."*
5. **HEADLINE + "LOGOSATZ"** — testimonial that ends with the brand name. *"Ich sorge für Ordnung in unserem Büro / Unsere Akten lagern bei BookaBox.com"*
6. **Wall typography** — at locations, single nouns or verbs at architectural scale: *"Move", "Help", "Deliver", "Wo Dinge wohnen", "Quadratmieter bei BookaBox.com"*
7. **Sentence A. Sentence B that reframes A.** *(pitch decks)*

## Do's and don'ts

### Do
- Pick one pole per piece and stay there
- Lead with a recognition question on Pole A surfaces — the brand's signature move
- Keep sentences short
- Use "Du" capitalized in German
- Spell the wordmark "BookaBox.com" every time
- Spell the social avatar "BaB" (B-a-B), never "BoB"
- Reuse phrasings from the brand story
- Let the headline carry the work; trim the body
- Headlines in Silka Bold (not Silka Black)
- For OOH/social hero pieces, default to Coral Pink `#F25966` as the campaign-color background
- For pitch decks, default to Coral Pink `#F25966` or Functional Orange `#FF9829` (Fund deck precedent)

### Don't
- Mix Pole A and Pole B in the same headline
- Write "Sie" in consumer-facing German
- Spell the wordmark wrong ("Bookabox", "BOOKABOX", "BookABox")
- Spell the social avatar "BoB" — it's "BaB"
- Invent claim variants ("Wir machen Dir Dein Leben einfacher")
- Use Silka Black where Silka Bold is the canonical choice
- Use empty intensifiers or corporate buzzwords
- Pile on adjectives — one strong word beats three weak ones
- Translate German idioms directly to English; rewrite in English's natural voice instead

## Translation notes (DE → EN)

The two poles map across languages but the patterns shift:

- "Du" → "you" (lowercase). The intimacy comes through tone and phrasing, not capitalization.
- Pole A in English leans into observational humor and short clauses, the same way the German does. *"When your closet's got room for one personality. Not two."*
- Pole B in English uses the same setup → reframe pattern as in the Fund deck. The German equivalents tend to be more verbose; English forces tighter phrasing.
- The claim does not translate verbatim. "Make your life easier" works informally; for the formal variant, "make life lighter" loses the promise. Better: don't fix an English claim — keep the spirit, vary the line per use.

## When unsure

If you cannot decide which pole a piece belongs to, ask **one** clarifying question before drafting:

> "Soll das in den emotionalen / pointierten Pol (wie *Dein Schrank ist zu klein für zwei Persönlichkeiten?*) oder in den beschreibenden / informativen Pol (wie *Smart. In 3 Klicks zur optimalen Lagerlösung.*)?"

Don't ask three questions. Don't draft both poles in parallel unless explicitly requested.
